My Music Gallery

I have been a loyal fan of American pop music for over a decade, since I was a child who barely know anything about the world. In these golden years of life, it was music that shaped my values, told me to be myself, and motivated me to explore about life and love relentlessly.

In my teenage years, I was fortunate enough to have lived in a blossom time of pop music, thanks to the pop stars around 2010. Since then, I have been actively expanding my boundaries in music appreciation; now I listen to all genres of music, from pop to indie rock, hip-hop and R&B.

Below are albums crucial to my aesthetic values and life stories. Tell me if I am lucky enough to share some common interests with you!

Norman F---ing Rockwell - Lana Del Rey

Lana Del Rey has been one of my favorite female songwriters since her debut in 2012. Different from her previous works that relied heavily on fancy production, somewhat sterotypical retrospective Americanism aesthetics, and her intricate vocal performance, Norman F---ing Rockewell returns to the most fundamental element of music - poetic songwriting. Collaborating with the gold star producer, Jack Antonoff, Lana successfully integrates beautiful melodies, California visuals, and political reflections, enticing her listeners to enjoy the moment with the California sunshine, ocean wave and palm trees, in such a disruptive time of human history.

Cut: Venice B--ch, The Greatest, Happiness Is A Butterfly

Ctrl - SZA

Getting to know SZA from the famous Rihanna collab, 'Consideration', off of the 8th album from the Fenty Beauty giant, I immediately found her the herald of my own heart - she sings what I think. Though SZA sings about the struggle of being a 'normal girl' and I share a different biological gender than her, the confusion and pain in self-identificication are universal to all human beings. She is outspoken about falling in love with men she shouldn't, dying to be the kind of girl that her man'd be proud to take home to Mama, pondering another life if she was meant to be a girl no different from the most ordinary ones. Everything that SZA sings ia about the inevitable puzzles thorughout everyone's personal growth, if they choose to experience that upsetting but meaningful process.

Cut: Love Galore, Normal Girl, Prom, 20 Something
